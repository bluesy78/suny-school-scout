SUNY Cobleskill

Known for outstanding programs in Agricultural Business, Culinary Arts, and Wildlife Management with a practical, hands-on approach.

  • Culinary Arts - American Culinary Federation accredited program.

üè† Housing Options
SUNY Cobleskill

Five residence halls with options from traditional dorms to suite-style living in a rural setting with close-knit community feel.

  • Traditional Residence Halls - Pearson, Fake, and Dix Halls offer standard double rooms with shared bathrooms.
  • Suite-Style Living - Vroman Hall features four-person suites with shared living areas.
  • Alumni Commons - Apartment-style housing with full kitchens for upperclassmen.
SUNY Oswego

13 residence halls including themed living communities and lakeside residence halls with stunning views of Lake Ontario.

  • Lakeside Halls - Scales, Waterbury, and Johnson Halls offer premium views of Lake Ontario.
  • Themed Living Communities - Special interest housing for honors, global studies, arts, and STEM.
  • The Village Townhouses - Modern apartment-style units with private bedrooms and full kitchens.
üé≠ Campus Life
SUNY Cobleskill

Intimate rural campus with strong agriculture and nature-focused activities and traditions. Over 50 clubs and organizations.

  • Student Clubs - Over 50 clubs with emphasis on agricultural and outdoor activities.

Vibrant lakeside campus with diverse cultural events and strong school spirit traditions. Over 200 clubs and organizations.

üåé Location & Environment
SUNY Cobleskill

Rural setting in the Schoharie Valley with a close-knit community atmosphere and natural surroundings.

  • Campus Setting - 782-acre campus with farm facilities and woodland areas.
  • Surrounding Area - Small town with easy access to outdoor recreation; 45 minutes from Albany.
  • Weather - Four distinct seasons with cold winters and mild summers.
SUNY Oswego

Lakeside campus with beautiful water views and a mix of suburban and small-city environment.

  • Campus Setting - 700-acre campus on Lake Ontario with over a mile of shoreline.
  • Surrounding Area - Small city with shopping and dining options; 40 minutes from Syracuse.
  • Weather - Four seasons with significant lake-effect snow in winter (100+ inches annually).
